Golf can be both a surprising entry and an obvious one, depending on your outlook. First off, it is one of the oldest sports in this list, having been established in the 13th Century in Scotland. It has successfully spread worldwide, and it now has 450 million fans across Europe, Asia, and America, which accounts for its vast reach.

Known for its graceful precise movements, this sport requires players to hit balls into small targets from long distances on a grassy surface known as a golf course. While many people may assume that this sport is only enjoyed by high-income individuals, this is not the case. People of all backgrounds and ages, from children to seniors enjoy golf.

Golf is a popular activity for millions, with an estimated fan base of 450 million people. The sport's appeal is genuinely global, with fans in Europe, Asia, America, and Canada enjoying both its serene and competitive sides. Golf draws people of various ability levels, from casual players to devoted pros.

Golf has become very popular around the world for several reasons. One big reason is that golf is a great way to get out into nature and enjoy the beautiful scenery while also getting some exercise. This combination of enjoying the outdoors and staying active makes golf appealing to many people.

The golf course is an idyllic landscape for leisure, but the game is also a popular spectator sport with roughly 400 million fans tuning in to the Masters and Opens tournaments. The compensation for top athletes is also on par with other sports leagues, with many of the top performers earning contracts for $100 million or more every year.
