American football (also known as football in the US and gridiron in other countries) is a sport in which two teams of eleven players compete on a rectangular field measuring 120 yards long by 53.3 yards wide with goalposts at both ends. The offense uses either running or passing to move an oval ball (the football) down the field. To receive a new set of four downs and continue the drive, they must progress it at least 10 yards in four downs; otherwise, the ball is turned over to the other team.

With an estimated 400 million fans, American football is gaining popularity globally. Its explosive action and tactical gameplay have attracted viewers around the world.

Rugby is a sport that combines elements of football, soccer, and American football. It requires speed, agility, strength, teamwork, and stamina. If you're new to rugby, focus on building these skills through regular practice and training. It's also important to learn the basic rules of the game and understand the different player positions.

The best way to start your rugby journey is by joining a local league or finding a training club in your area. This will help you learn from experienced players and improve your skills with their guidance.
